Diospyros virginiana The American Persimmon

The History of the Persimmon. The American Persimmon is native to the eastern part os America. When ripe, the persimmon is a sweet little fruit. Not only does it taste delicious it contains nutrients such as vitamin C, calcium, iron and potassium. This fruit was known to be eaten by the Native Americans and the first europeans to arrive to the Americas. Settlers learned to make breads, pudding, syrup and pies. During the civil war, soldiers used persimmon seeds to replace buttons. In addition the american persimmon could be used for medicinal purposes, to make beer, brandy and a coffee substitute. Colonialist also found the wood from the Persimmon tree to be useful.
